## Unified Takeaways

- Separate content intelligence from rendering. First make a storyboard and evidence ledger; then choose PPTX, HTML, or image cards.
- Treat editability as a route decision. PPTX should use editable shapes/text/charts; image-card output may rasterize composition intentionally.
- Use HTML when interaction, keyboard shortcuts, responsive presentation, or presenter utilities matter more than native PowerPoint editing.
- Use image cards when fast reading, social sharing, or a strongly illustrated style matters more than slide editability.
- Borrow taste patterns, not surface decoration: grid, typography, pacing, evidence density, and presentation mechanics.
- Keep narration and speaker support as first-class deck outputs: notes, transcript, rehearsal timing, and optional audio plan.

## Safety And Rights

- Do not bypass paywalls, login walls, bot checks, or source access controls while gathering deck material.
- Do not clone or synthesize a person's voice without explicit consent and a legally provided sample.
- Do not copy source project templates, screenshots, or distinctive art into a user deliverable unless the license and use case permit it.
- Prefer citing sources in speaker notes or appendix for research-heavy decks.
